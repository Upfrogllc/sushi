$5.9 Million in Prize Money

Championship Record

Sushi is the only vessel in history to win both the White Marlin Open and the Big Rock Blue Marlin Tournament — two of the most prestigious billfish competitions in the world. With over $7 million in total tournament earnings and more than 10 wins or placements across the East Coast circuit, Captain Charley Pereira has earned his place among the all-time greats of offshore sportfishing.

2021
$3,200,000
White Marlin Open · Ocean City, MD · 1st Place Overall

85.5 lb White Marlin caught by angler Butch Wright on the final day of the tournament. At $37,000 per pound, it was the most valuable fish in WMO history at the time. When the fish went to the scales, Charley stayed on the boat, turned away, and prayed. He heard the crowd roar before anyone told him they'd won.

2023
$2,700,000
65th Annual Big Rock Blue Marlin Tournament · Morehead City, NC

484.5 lb Blue Marlin. With this win, Sushi became the only boat in history to claim both the WMO and Big Rock titles. Charley's reaction at the scales was the same as 2021: he stayed on the boat, looked toward the ocean, and prayed.

2015
419.1 lbs
Big Rock Blue Marlin Tournament · Big Rock Landing

Blue Marlin weighed at Big Rock Landing. Angler: Josh Barnes. Captain: Charley Pereira. A top placement that established Sushi as a consistent contender at the highest level.

2013
$573,850
40th Annual White Marlin Open · Ocean City, MD · 1st Place Tuna

276 lb Bigeye Tuna caught on the final day of the tournament by angler Jim Czaban. Another last-day comeback for Team Sushi.
