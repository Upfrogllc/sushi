57' Custom Island Boat Works

The Sushi

Built to Win

The Sushi isn't just a charter boat — she's a purpose-built tournament machine, designed and constructed by Captain Charley Pereira and his team at Island Boat Works in Wanchese, North Carolina.

Every decision in the build was driven by one question: what does it take to get offshore first, find the fish, and bring them to the scales? Twin CAT C32 diesels paired with ZF 2070A transmissions and SmartCommand controls push the Sushi to 55 mph. 820 gallons of fuel capacity means routine 80-mile offshore runs and the range to fish the far edges of the Gulf Stream when other boats can't.

Below deck, the Sushi is unlike any other charter vessel. The custom teak salon features eight leather reclining home-theater seats, full air conditioning, a complete galley with refrigerator and microwave, and a DVD entertainment system. Three staterooms and two heads make overnight trips comfortable for crew and clients alike.

The exterior is built for work: a custom teak cockpit, tournament-grade rod holders, and the signature transom lettering that's become one of the most recognizable transoms in East Coast sportfishing.
