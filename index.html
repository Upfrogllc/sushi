Owner & Captain

Charley Pereira

“I turn away from the scales and I pray. I can't control it. The best thing for me to do is stay on the boat, look away, and pray.”

Charley grew up at the docks in Coconut Grove, Florida, eight years old with a bicycle trailer hauling ice for the local fishermen. He went on to earn an aeronautical engineering degree, investigate black-box crashes for the NTSB, and work nights and weekends until he could buy his first boat. He moved to the Outer Banks in 2005, built the Sushi with his own hands, and became the only captain in history to win both the White Marlin Open and Big Rock.

When asked his secret, Charley credits his faith, his family, and the mentors who shaped him along the way.

In Memory

Savannah Caroline Pereira

April 30, 2009 – March 9, 2010

In 2010, Charley and Lisa lost their ten-month-old daughter Savannah to a tragic accident. Rather than retreat, Charley became a national advocate for child product safety, helping drive new federal standards for baby monitors. They established the Savannah Memorial Fund through the Cade Foundation — a grant providing fertility treatment assistance to families in need. The fund has raised over $142,000 and helped bring eight new lives into the world.

“I would give up my life to have her back. She was everything we could have hoped for.”

— Charley Pereira
